The Strategic Advantage

The dynamics you can't see decide the deal.

Philosophy

Beyond tactics. Understanding people.

Most people focus on what is said across the negotiation table. I focus on what isn't. Behind every position are interests, needs, concerns, and strategic considerations — the ability to identify them is what makes it possible to build better solutions, establish trust, and achieve outcomes that hold over time.

“I don't teach people how to win a negotiation. I teach them how to understand people, build trust, and succeed over time.” Dr. Hilla Dotan-Dykstein
Principle 01

People are systems, not positions

People are systems of interests, incentives, and history — not positions. Understanding these systems is the foundation of every successful negotiation.

Principle 02

Trust is a strategic asset

Trust is not a by-product — it is a strategic asset. Long-term relationships consistently outperform short-term transactional gains.

Principle 03

Ethics is a competitive advantage

Ethics is not a constraint — it is a competitive advantage. Sustainable influence is built on credibility, consistency, and integrity.
